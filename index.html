The Definition

A "side" is a gay or bisexual man who does not prefer anal sex. That's the core definition. Everything else is personal preference.

Sides enjoy other forms of intimacy — oral sex, mutual masturbation, frottage (rubbing together), cuddling, kissing, massage, and emotional connection. Some sides are open to anal occasionally. Some never do it. Some haven't decided yet. All are valid.

"Side" was created by sex therapist Dr. Joe Kort to give men a word that describes their experience without forcing them into the top/bottom binary.

Being a side isn't about being scared, inexperienced, or prude. It's about knowing what you like and being honest about it. It's about consent and preference. It's about refusing to let someone else's framework define your sexuality.

Myths vs. Reality

"Sides are just closeted or in denial." Sides are confident in their preferences. They know what they want and aren't afraid to say it.

"No one wants to date a side." Sides date other sides, open-minded partners, and versatile men. The dating pool is real and growing.
"Sides are asexual." Sides are sexual. They enjoy sex — just not anal penetration. This is different from asexuality.

What does side mean in gay relationships? +
In gay relationships, a side is someone who prefers sexual intimacy that does not involve anal penetration. Sides may enjoy oral sex, manual stimulation, frottage, cuddling, kissing, and deep emotional connection. Being a side is about preference, not inexperience or fear.
